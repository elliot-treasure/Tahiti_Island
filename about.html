About Taniti

Taniti is a small tropical island that covers less than 500 square miles and is located in the Pacific Ocean. Taniti is known for it's diverse landscape that includes both sandy and rocky beaches, lush tropical rainforests, all wrapping a mountainous interior that encircles a small active volcano. 

Home to approximately 20,000 indigenous residents, the island of Taniti's resident's hold a unique and rich culture. Although it is now reliant on the increased tourism industry, the economy used to focus on fishing and agriculture. Today Taniti offers a unique blend of natural attractions, local traditions, and provides this with the convenience of modern amenities, making this a welcoming destination for travelers while also preserving it's unique charm and history of the island's community.

General Information

Taniti is a small tropical island located in the Pacific Ocean. 

Taniti cover's less than 500 square miles. 

Many younger Tanitian's speak fluent English; However, very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by the older residents. Facilities, such as hospitals, have many multilingual employees. 

Taniti primarily uses the US Dollar, but many business also accept euros and yen. Several banks offer currency exchange services and several businesses accept all major credit cards.

The standard outlet is 120 volts, matching what is commonly used in the United States. 

Violent crime is very rare, but as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes.

There is one hospital and several clinics.

Cuisine

The legal drinking age is 18, but it is not strictly enforced. 

Alcohol is served and sold between 9am and midnight. 

Taniti currently has 10 restaurants with five of them serving local dishes featuring fish and rice, three serving American-style meals, and two serving Pan-Asian cuisine. 

Taniti has two supermarkets, two smaller grocery stores, and one convenience store that's open 24/7. 

Transportation

Almost all visitors arrive to Taniti by air while other's arrive by a small cruise ship that docks in Yellow Leaf Bay. 

Public busses run in Taniti City from 5am to 11pm every day, along with Taxi's. Private busses are also available to shuttle to the rest of the island. Rental cards can be rented from local rental agencies near the airport. Otherwise bicycles can be rented from numerous vendors, but helmets are also included in the rental fee as it is required by law to wear them. Lastly, Taniti City is fairly flat and is easy to navigate just by walking. Many visitors staying in the Merriton Landing area prefer to travel on foot.
